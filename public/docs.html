Clarity Scan Documentation

About Clarity Scan

Clarity Scan is a Chrome Extension designed to analyze YouTube content and detect "Brain Rot" content by processing video transcripts.

What is Brain-Rot?

A brain-rot YouTube video is one that prioritizes sensationalism, misinformation, or mindless entertainment over substance, critical thinking, or meaningful value.

Clarity Scan helps detect such content, allowing users to make informed decisions about the media they consume.

Brain-Rot Scoring System

Clarity Scan assigns a Brain-Rot Score ranging from 0% to 100%, in increments of 10%:

  • 0%: No brain-rot is present. The videos are purely academic or educational in nature with high-quality and informative content.
  • 10%: Educational videos that are more casual and include more personality. These may include personal anecdotes or small jokes.
  • 20%: More casual educational videos where the presence of humor may begin to make the video less straightforward.
  • 30%: Casual videos that are on topic, but frequent tangents, skits, or stories interrupt the flow.
  • 40%: Videos that begin to use slightly exaggerated content, memes, or jokes, which alienate viewers looking for serious content.
  • 50%: Videos where memes or jokes are still creative, funny, or charming, but detract from the main focus of the video.
  • 60%: Videos where humorous or silly content clashes with and threatens to overtake more serious topics in the video.
  • 70%: Videos that contain more exaggerated elements than actual substance, distracting from the main topic of the video.
  • 80%: Videos that make overly emotional, dramatic, and hyperbolic claims about topics.
  • 90%: Videos that make ludicrous and baseless claims about topics.
  • 100%: Videos which are completely unintelligible, misinformed, and wrong.

The score is calculated using AI-driven content analysis alongside our custom Python model.

How We Grade Brain-Rot

We use a multi-step process to analyze YouTube video content:

  1. We first detect and retrieve the transcript from the current YouTube video.
  2. We define brain-rot as: “A brain-rot YouTube video is one that prioritizes sensationalism, misinformation, or mindless entertainment over substance, critical thinking, or meaningful value.”
  3. We then prompt ChatGPT to generate a short brain-rot review based on the transcript.
  4. To assign a score consistent with our scoring system, we feed this review into our custom Python model.
  5. Our model is trained on a combination of ~1000 real and synthetically generated YouTube video reviews that have been manually labeled.
  6. It uses a TF-IDF vectorizer to extract features from the review based on word frequencies.
  7. These features are fed into a 3-layer neural network, which assigns a final brain-rot score.
  8. Finally, we send back the score to the user along with the ChatGPT-generated review, providing further clarification.

Features

  • Analyzes YouTube videos in real-time.
  • Detects low-quality or misleading content.
  • Uses both AI analysis and a Python-based scoring model.
  • Provides an intuitive UI for reviewing results.

How to Use

  1. Install Clarity Scan from Github.
  2. Open any YouTube video.
  3. Click on the Clarity Scan icon in the extension bar.
  4. Review the analysis results in the popup.
